Level Design

For me, level design is the art of crafting experiences. I believe a great level tells a story through its environment, guides the player intuitively, and creates memorable challenges through careful pacing and layout. My designs prioritize player flow, strategic sightlines, and environmental storytelling.

Map: 'Aetheria District' (Cybernetic Echo)

A multiplayer map designed for a 5v5 team deathmatch mode. The goal was to create a fast-paced, asymmetrical map with significant verticality to complement the game's advanced movement mechanics.

Key Features:

  • Vertical Gameplay: The map features multiple levels connected by rooftops, zip-lines, and wall-runnable surfaces, encouraging players to think in three dimensions.
  • Strategic Chokepoints: A central plaza acts as a high-value chokepoint, while narrow back alleys offer risky but rewarding flanking routes.
  • Environmental Storytelling: The level is littered with clues about the district's lore, from holographic graffiti depicting gang conflicts to abandoned market stalls.

Dungeon: The Sunken Vault (Hell's Gate)

A procedurally generated dungeon floor with a focus on exploration and puzzle-solving. The design centers around a central vault that the player must find three keys to unlock, with each key hidden in a unique wing of the dungeon.

Key Features:

  • Procedural Room Templates: Designed over 30 unique room templates with varied combat encounters, traps, and light puzzles that the procedural generator uses to build the level.
  • Gated Progression: Each of the three wings has a unique environmental theme (Flooded, Overgrown, Crystalline) and a mini-boss that must be defeated to acquire a key.
  • Dynamic Pacing: The generation algorithm ensures a clear pacing curve, starting with simple encounters and gradually introducing more complex threats as the player delves deeper.
